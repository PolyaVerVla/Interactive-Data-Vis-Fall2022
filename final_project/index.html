U.S. Food Imports and U.S. Immigration

Americans are ethnically diverse and it affects the food choices that people make which shifts altogether with migration change. Historically, the U.S. has been considered one of the easiest countries to immigrate to, and according to the UN Department of Economics and Social Affairs, as of mid-2020 the United States has the highest immigrant population in the world at 50.6 million. The reasons for immigration can vary and depend on many factors such as seeking improved career opportunities, reuniting with family, better access to healthcare or education, escape from military conflicts, etc. The data from the U.S. Department of Agriculture shows that the imports of edible products have changed in the last thirty years and the set of foods nowadays includes more tropical products, spices, and imported gourmet products than before. Also, the USA imports some products throughout the whole year, such as cocoa and coffee. Some imported products go to agricultural-processing industries based in the United States for further export to third countries. I wanted to see if the trade had any correlation with immigration between 2017 and 2020. In order to do that, I will select the 6 countries with the highest import values and the 6 countries with the highest number of immigrants during that time period and visualize these relationships.

There are two countries, the Philippines and Mexico, whose high food import value correlates with the high number of immigrants. Three countries France, Italy and Singapore which have a low food import value correlates with low numbers of immigrants. Canada has one of the highest food import values but a low number of immigrants; and China, India and the Dominican Republic have a low food import value but high numbers of immigrants. This tendency almost hasn't changed between 2017 and 2020. However there is a significant drop in the number of immigrants in 2020 from all countries due to the pandemic, while the food import value from all the countries almost hasn't changed since 2019.
