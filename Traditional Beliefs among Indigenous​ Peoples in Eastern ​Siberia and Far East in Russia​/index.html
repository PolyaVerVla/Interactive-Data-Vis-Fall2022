Some Information :

    Over 1.5 million in Russia are Indigenous Siberians​
    Over 40 ethnic groups (19 of them dominantly adhere to traditional beliefs) ​​
    Ten language groups (4 of them are isolates)​​

There are two groups, Ainu and Kamchadals, which are still not recognized as indigenous groups today. Ainu mostly live in Japan (mainly on Hokkaido Island), however there are some groups in Russia, in Sakhalin Oblast. Kamchadals are an ethnic group that resulted from the international marriages between Russian settlers and local indigenous peoples in the region of Kamchatka Krai.

Four isolate language groups included in these indigenous populations are in a danger of extinction: Ainu, Yeniseian, Nivkh and Yukaghir. Some scholars consider Yukaghir as related to the Uralic language family. [1]

The original religions of the Indigenous groups in Siberia were traditional beliefs, mainly Tengrism, Shamanism, and Animism. Notably, there are more women among shamans further from urban areas in Siberia. Some scholars believe that in urban areas shamanism might be more influenced by the nearby cultures, making those traditional beliefs more patriarchal. [2]

However due to Chinese, Central Asian, and Russian influence, nowadays other religions are also widely spread in the region, especially in urbanized areas in the southern part of Siberia - for instance, Buddhism for Buryats and Tuvans, Islam among Siberian Tatars, and Orthodox Christianity among the rest.
